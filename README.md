Dario Rodighiero is a designer working at the intersection of digital humanities, information design, and science communication. His practice turns open-access archives into visual form, opening them to interpretation and understanding. Two strands organize the work. The first is the mapping of science, developed through organizational charts in [Mapping Affinities](https://library.oapen.org/handle/20.500.12657/91793) and extended to artificial intelligence in *Grounding AI Map*. The second engages cultural heritage, with projects such as *Surprise Machines* and the *Map of the Analogous City*. Across both strands, knowledge design operates as a research methodology: a way of seeing, and a means of experimenting with how inquiry takes form.

At the [University of Groningen](https://www.rug.nl/), he is Assistant Professor of Science and Technology Studies at the interdisciplinary faculty [Campus Fryslân](https://www.rug.nl/cf/). There he coordinates the minor [Data Wise: Data Science in Society](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/minors/data-wise/) and teaches data and visual literacy in the Bachelor's program [Data Science and Society](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/bachelor/bsc-data-science-and-society/). He maintains active collaborations with Harvard University as faculty associate at the [Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society](https://cyber.harvard.edu/) and principal at [metaLAB](https://metalabharvard.github.io/) (at) Harvard/Berlin/Basel, a research and teaching center dedicated to experimenting with digital technologies in the arts and humanities. He is co-chair of VISAP, the IEEE VIS Arts Program, for the 2025 edition in Vienna and the 2026 edition in Boston.

<!-- split -->

Dario holds a PhD from the [EPFL](https://www.epfl.ch/), where he attended the doctoral program Architecture and Sciences of the City. He has held research and teaching positions at [MIT](https://www.mit.edu/), the [European Commission](https://commission.europa.eu/), [Paris-Sorbonne University](https://www.sorbonne-universite.fr/), and [Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/), and was visiting professor at [FU Berlin](https://www.fu-berlin.de/) and [Paris Nanterre University](https://www.parisnanterre.fr/). The collaboration with Bruno Latour at the [médialab](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/) shaped his engagement with digital platforms as tools for philosophical inquiry and exploration. He lectured at venues such as [CERN](https://home.cern/) and [Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/), and exhibited his work at the [MAXXI](https://www.maxxi.art/) and [Harvard Art Museums](https://harvardartmuseums.org/), reflecting a sustained commitment to public engagement and interdisciplinary collaboration.
