Dario Rodighiero is a designer working at the intersection of digital humanities, information design, and science communication. His practice turns open-access archives into visual form, opening them to interpretation and understanding. Two strands organize the work. The first is the mapping of science, developed through organizational charts in [*Mapping Affinities*](/mapping-affinities-democratizing-data-visualization) and extended to artificial intelligence in [*Grounding AI Map*](/grounding-ai-map-the-consequences-of-living-with-the-trouble-of-an-irreductionist-map). The second engages cultural heritage, with projects such as [*Surprise Machines*](/surprise-machines-revealing-harvard-art-museums-image-collection) and the [*Map of the Analogous City*](/the-analogous-city-the-map). Across both strands, knowledge design operates as a research methodology: a way of seeing, and a means of experimenting with how inquiry takes form.

At the [University of Groningen](https://www.rug.nl/), he is Assistant Professor of Science and Technology Studies at the interdisciplinary faculty [Campus Fryslân](https://www.rug.nl/cf/). There he coordinates the minor [Data Wise: Data Science in Society](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/minors/data-wise/) and teaches data and visual literacy in the Bachelor's program [Data Science and Society](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/bachelor/bsc-data-science-and-society/). He maintains active collaborations with Harvard University as a faculty associate at the [Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society](https://cyber.harvard.edu/) and a principal at [metaLAB](https://metalabharvard.github.io/) (at) Harvard/Berlin/Basel, a research and teaching center dedicated to experimenting with digital technologies in the arts and humanities. He is co-chair of [VISAP](https://visap.net/), the IEEE VIS Arts Program, for the 2025 and 2026 editions, in Vienna and Boston.

<!-- split -->

Dario holds a PhD from the [École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)](https://www.epfl.ch/), where he attended the doctoral program in Architecture and Sciences of the City. He held research and teaching positions at [MIT](https://www.mit.edu/), the [European Commission](https://commission.europa.eu/), [Paris-Sorbonne University](https://www.sorbonne-universite.fr/), and [Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/) — including the [médialab](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/), where his engagement with digital platforms as tools for philosophical inquiry took shape — and was visiting professor at [FU Berlin](https://www.fu-berlin.de/) and [Paris Nanterre University](https://www.parisnanterre.fr/). Dario lectured at venues such as [CERN](https://home.cern/) and [Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/), and exhibited his work at [MAXXI](https://www.maxxi.art/) and the [Harvard Art Museums](https://harvardartmuseums.org/), reflecting a sustained commitment to public engagement and interdisciplinary collaboration.
