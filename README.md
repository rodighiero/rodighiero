Dario Rodighiero is an Assistant Professor of Science and Technology Studies at the [University of Groningen](https://www.rug.nl/), where he is involved in programs that bridge data science with society. Based at the interdisciplinary faculty [Campus Fryslân](https://www.rug.nl/cf/), he coordinates the minor [Data Wise](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/minors/data-wise/) and teaches data and visual literacy within the [Data Science and Society](https://www.rug.nl/cf/education/bachelor/bsc-data-science-and-society/) Bachelor's program. He maintains active collaborations with Harvard University, where he is a faculty associate at the [Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society](https://cyber.harvard.edu/) and a principal at [metaLAB](https://metalabharvard.github.io/)—a research and teaching laboratory dedicated to experimenting with digital technologies in the arts and humanities.

Combining computational techniques with design, Dario investigates how complex information can be revealed. Grounded in Science and Technology Studies, his research focuses on the mapping of science: he is the author of [*Mapping Affinities: Democratizing Data Visualization*](https://dariorodighiero.com/Mapping-Affinities), which proposes new ways to design organizational charts. His work further engages with digital cultural archives, exploring questions of representation, interpretation, and self-identification. Visualization is approached as a method for knowledge design, bridging critical inquiry and design practice to foster reflection and dialogue by opening new visual modes of understanding.

Dario holds a PhD from the [École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL)](https://www.epfl.ch/), where he attended the doctoral program Architecture and Sciences of the City. He has held research and teaching positions at [MIT](https://www.mit.edu/), the [European Commission](https://commission.europa.eu/), [Paris-Sorbonne University](https://www.sorbonne-universite.fr/), and [Sciences Po](https://www.sciencespo.fr/). The collaboration with Bruno Latour at the [médialab](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/) shaped his engagement with digital platforms as tools for philosophical inquiry and collective exploration. He lectured at venues such as [CERN](https://home.cern/) and [Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/), and exhibited his work at the [MAXXI](https://www.maxxi.art/) and [Harvard Art Museums](https://harvardartmuseums.org/), reflecting a sustained commitment to public engagement and interdisciplinary collaboration.
